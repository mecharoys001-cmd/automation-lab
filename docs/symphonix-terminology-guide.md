# Symphonix Scheduler — Terminology Guide

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-10  
**Purpose:** Clarify the difference between Event Templates, Weekly Templates (Schedule Builder), and the Calendar

---

## The Three Core Concepts

### 1. **Event Templates** (Building Blocks)
📍 **Location:** Admin → Event Templates

**What it is:**  
Individual class/session templates — the building blocks of your schedule.

**Examples:**
- "Grade 3 Piano" (Tuesday, 9:00 AM, 45 minutes, Ms. Johnson, Classroom 101)
- "Strings Ensemble" (Thursday, 1:00 PM, 60 minutes, Mr. Davis, Stage)
- "K-1 Movement Class" (Monday/Wednesday, 10:30 AM, 30 minutes)

**Key Fields:**
- Subject (e.g., Piano, Strings, Percussion)
- Grade level
- Instructor
- Day(s) of week
- Time slot
- Venue
- Duration
- Tags

**Purpose:**  
Define WHAT sessions happen, WHO teaches them, WHERE they happen, and WHEN (day/time).

**Important:**  
Event Templates do NOT create actual sessions on the calendar yet — they're just blueprints.

---

### 2. **Weekly Template / Schedule Builder** (The Weekly Plan)
📍 **Location:** Admin → Schedule Builder

**What it is:**  
A visual weekly grid where you place and organize your Event Templates to build your ideal week.

**What you see:**
- 5-day grid (Monday–Friday)
- Time slots from 8 AM to 4 PM (configurable)
- Draggable blocks representing Event Templates
- Multi-venue lanes (shows events side-by-side if you have multiple venues)

**What you do here:**
- Drag templates from the sidebar onto specific days/times
- Visualize conflicts (overlapping instructors, double-booked venues)
- Fine-tune the weekly schedule before generating real sessions
- Adjust time ranges for each day
- Configure repeating patterns (e.g., "every 2 weeks" for A/B schedules)

**Purpose:**  
Plan and visualize your IDEAL WEEK before creating actual calendar sessions.

**Key Actions:**
- **Save** — Saves your template placements (does NOT create sessions yet)
- **Create Schedule** — Generates actual calendar sessions based on this weekly plan

**Important:**  
The Schedule Builder is a PLANNING TOOL. Nothing you do here affects the actual calendar until you click "Create Schedule."

---

### 3. **Calendar** (The Actual Schedule)
📍 **Location:** Admin → Calendar

**What it is:**  
The real schedule — actual dated sessions that staff and students see.

**What you see:**
- Month/week/day views of scheduled sessions
- Each session has a specific DATE (e.g., Tuesday, October 15, 2024)
- Draft, Published, and Cancelled sessions
- Filters by instructor, grade, subject, venue

**How sessions get here:**
1. You build Event Templates
2. You arrange them in the Schedule Builder (weekly grid)
3. You click **"Create Schedule"** in the Schedule Builder
4. The system auto-generates dated sessions across your program's date range (e.g., September 1 – June 30)
5. Those sessions appear on the Calendar

**Editing sessions:**
- You can edit individual sessions (change time, instructor, venue)
- You can publish/unpublish sessions
- You can delete or cancel sessions
- Changes are session-specific (editing one session doesn't change the template)

**Publishing:**
- Draft sessions = internal only
- Published sessions = visible to staff/students (if you have a public view)

**Important:**  
The Calendar shows INSTANCES of your templates across specific dates. Changing a template doesn't retroactively change existing sessions — you'd need to regenerate.

## The Workflow (How It All Connects)

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Create Event Templates**  
   → Define your classes (Piano, Strings, etc.)  
   → Add instructors, venues, time slots  
   → These are your building blocks

2. **Build Your Weekly Schedule**  
   → Go to Schedule Builder  
   → Drag templates onto the weekly grid  
   → Visualize and resolve conflicts  
   → Save your layout

3. **Generate the Calendar**  
   → In Schedule Builder, click **"Create Schedule"**  
   → System generates dated sessions for every week in your program  
   → Sessions appear on the Calendar page

4. **Manage & Publish**  
   → Go to Calendar  
   → Review generated sessions  
   → Make adjustments if needed  
   → Publish sessions when ready

## Common Questions

### Q: If I edit an Event Template, does it update existing sessions on the Calendar?
**A:** No. Event Templates are blueprints. Once you generate sessions, they're independent. If you change a template and want the calendar updated, you'd need to regenerate (or manually edit sessions).

### Q: What's the difference between "Save" and "Create Schedule" in the Schedule Builder?
**A:**  
- **Save** = Saves your template placements on the weekly grid (no calendar sessions created)
- **Create Schedule** = Generates real dated sessions on the Calendar based on your saved layout

### Q: Can I have multiple weekly templates (e.g., Week A / Week B)?
**A:** Yes! Event Templates support "Repeats Every X Weeks" — you can build alternating schedules (e.g., a 2-week rotation).

### Q: What if I delete a session on the Calendar?
**A:** It only deletes that specific instance. The template remains intact. If you regenerate, the session will come back unless you remove/modify the template.

### Q: Can I manually add sessions to the Calendar without using templates?
**A:** Currently, the Calendar is generated FROM templates. You can edit generated sessions, but you can't create standalone calendar events. (This might be a future feature request.)

## Naming Recommendations

To reduce confusion, consider these naming improvements:

| Current Name | Clearer Alternative | Why |
|--------------|---------------------|-----|
| Schedule Builder | **Weekly Template Builder** | "Schedule" is ambiguous — this clarifies it's the weekly plan |
| Calendar | **Session Calendar** or **Schedule Calendar** | Emphasizes these are real dated sessions |
| Event Templates | **Class Templates** or **Session Templates** | "Event" is generic — "Class" or "Session" is more specific to schools |

## Quick Reference

| Term | What It Is | Where You Manage It | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| **Event Template** | Blueprint for a recurring class | Event Templates page | Define WHAT/WHO/WHERE/WHEN |
| **Weekly Template** | Weekly grid layout | Schedule Builder page | Visualize and plan your week |
| **Calendar / Schedule** | Real dated sessions | Calendar page | Actual schedule with specific dates |

---

## Key Takeaway

Think of it like building with LEGO:

1. **Event Templates** = Individual LEGO bricks (defined but not placed)
2. **Schedule Builder** = Blueprint/instruction manual (shows where bricks go)
3. **Calendar** = The finished LEGO structure (actual built thing with specific placement)

You design the bricks → arrange them in a plan → build the final structure.
